BASIS Oro Valley High School

High School (2013 - 2017)

I went to this small charter high school in Oro Valley, AZ for both high school and middle school. It is somewhat famous for "improving" the quality of education in Arizona, by implementing a European model of education that focuses on rewarding academic achievement, allowing students to accelerate their learning through advanced classes and electives, and generally promoting a culture of hard work. In practice, however, the pressure causes many students to drop out and move to public schools, effectively selecting for students that either naturally excel in academic fields or thrive under pressure. The good results of the school are, in part, a result of this.

Research Assistant

Part-Time Employment (2020-2021)

Starting during my undergraduate degree with my honor's thesis and finishing during my Master's degree, I worked with a statistical computing and biophysics lab. During my time in the lab, I researched and implemented methods for Bayesian inference on models of biophysical systems labelled with Förster resonance energy transfer (FRET). The novelty was based on the application of Bayesian Nonparametrics in order to relax the need for an existing model during data analysis. Instead, the number of model states and the transitions between them could be inferred directly from the data. The research resulted in a three part series of paper on Bayesian Nonparametrics:

  • Safar, M., Saurabh, A., Sarkar, B., Fazel, M., Ishii, K., Tahara, T., Sgouralis, I., & Pressé, S. (2022). Single-photon smFRET. III. Application to pulsed illumination. Biophysical Reports, 2(4), 100088. DOI

BNP-FRET Papers

During my time at Arizona State University, I collaborated with a statistical computing and biophysics research group that specialized in applying Bayesian nonparametric methods to problems in biophysics. This approach was successful primarily because the systems being observed often had very little available data per experiment or had very significant sources of error that could be modeled and accounted for (such as the effects of optical equipment).

My collaboration implemented this approach for experiments that utilized Förster resonance energy transfer (FRET) in order to monitor the distance between two points on an individual molecule. Because we were monitoring a single molecule, photons came one at a time, and there was a relatively short timeframe before the dyes were chemically altered ("burnt out") by the lasers. Combining this with the desire to learn the underlying number of distinct spatial configurations taken by the molecule made it an ideal problem for Bayesian nonparametrics.

The resulting papers, published sometime after I finished my master's degree, were:

  • Safar, M., Saurabh, A., Sarkar, B., Fazel, M., Ishii, K., Tahara, T., Sgouralis, I., & Pressé, S. (2022). Single-photon smFRET. III. Application to pulsed illumination. Biophysical Reports, 2(4), 100088. DOI
  • Saurabh, A., Fazel, M., Safar, M., Sgouralis, I., & Pressé, S. (2023). Single-photon smFRET. I: Theory and conceptual basis. Biophysical Reports, 3(1), 100089. DOI
  • Saurabh, A., Safar, M., Fazel, M., Sgouralis, I., & Pressé, S. (2023). Single-photon smFRET: II. Application to continuous illumination. Biophysical Reports, 3(1), 100087. DOI

Game Dev Exploration

I have always had an artistic fascination with games, as I'm sure many people in my generation do. The rapid onset of computers, and their power turned games from clunky text-based interfaces to beautifully rendered 3D environments in less than half a century. But this innovation is not so important as the innovation that came with people's attempts to use the technology. The language of video game mechanics has blossomed in this newfound limelight, and I am not immune to the excitement that comes with seeing a completely new form of human expression gain its first legs.

While I am unfortunately fairly busy and not particularly skilled when it comes to drawing, sculpting, or audio design, I would like to try this form of art.

My earliest project was in a small game jam in 2020 where I made a small game with two other amateurs. You can find it here on itch.io.

I will keep prototyping and hopefully produce something satisfying in the future.
